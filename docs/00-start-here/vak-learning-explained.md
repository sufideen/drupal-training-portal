# VAK Learning Styles — Explained for Drupal Learners

VAK stands for **Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic**.
It describes how people prefer to take in and process new information.
Most people use a mix of all three, but usually one or two dominate.

---

## 👁 Visual Learners

**You prefer:** Seeing information — diagrams, charts, colour, layout, spatial organisation.

**Signs you are a visual learner:**
- You understand a diagram faster than a paragraph of text
- You draw mind maps or boxes-and-arrows when planning
- You notice when something "looks wrong" on a page

**How this guide serves you:**
- Architecture diagrams at the start of every major lesson
- Folder tree visualisations before any file is created
- Before/after screenshots of the Drupal admin panel
- Colour-coded code blocks that distinguish commands from output

**Tip:** Start every lesson by studying the diagram at the top.
Build a mental map of where everything sits before reading the steps.

---

## 🔊 Auditory Learners

**You prefer:** Hearing or reading information — explanations, logic, reasoning, spoken walkthroughs.

**Signs you are an auditory learner:**
- You understand something better after someone explains it to you
- You talk through problems out loud
- You prefer a tutorial video over a diagram

**How this guide serves you:**
- Every lesson has a "Understand It" section written as a spoken walkthrough
- Concepts are explained with analogies and plain language before jargon is introduced
- Command sequences are explained line by line, not just listed

**Tip:** Read the "Auditory" sections out loud, or use your OS text-to-speech.
Hearing the words reinforces retention for auditory learners.

---

## ✋ Kinesthetic Learners

**You prefer:** Doing — hands-on practice, trial and error, building things yourself.

**Signs you are a kinesthetic learner:**
- You learn faster by trying than by reading
- You feel frustrated by long explanations before you can "just do something"
- You remember what you did, not what you read

**How this guide serves you:**
- Every lesson has a "Do It" exercise you complete on your own machine
- Exercises build a real working site, not toy examples
- Checklists let you track what you have completed
- Scripts automate repetitive setup so you spend time on the parts that matter

**Tip:** Jump straight to the "Kinesthetic" section.
Do the exercise first. Then read the explanation to understand what just happened.

---

## Finding Your Mix

Most Drupal admins and developers are **Kinesthetic-Visual** —
they want to see the structure and then build it.
Most Drupal developers who come from a programming background are
**Kinesthetic-Auditory** — they want to understand the logic and then code it.

There is no wrong combination. Use whichever sections help you most.
